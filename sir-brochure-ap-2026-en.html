What is SIR

Special Intensive Revision — Introduction

"Special Intensive Revision" (SIR) is a door-to-door process of thoroughly verifying and updating the electoral roll, conducted by the Election Commission of India (ECI). As per currently available official information, this process is scheduled in Andhra Pradesh.

This process covers all districts and constituencies in Andhra Pradesh. For your area's details, contact your local BLO or visit ceoandhra.nic.in.

What is the objective?

  • Verify that every name on the roll is genuine.
  • Remove names of deceased persons, those permanently relocated, and duplicate entries.
  • Add names of eligible voters not yet on the roll.
  • Prepare a clean and accurate electoral roll.

Benefits for the voter

Name Security

Your name and details are verified. Any errors can be corrected.

Free Process

Filling and submitting the Enumeration Form is completely free. No payment to anyone is required.

Remember: SIR is only an electoral roll revision. It is NOT NRC, and it is NOT a citizenship process. Filling the form has no effect on your citizenship.

What to Do

Steps You Need to Take

1 · Collect the Form

Your BLO will visit your home and provide a pre-filled Enumeration Form (EF). If the BLO hasn't visited, you can access it online at voters.eci.gov.in.

2 · Verify Your Details

Check that your name, father's/husband's name, age, address, and photo are correct. Make corrections if needed.

3 · Sign and Submit

Sign the form and return it to the BLO. Make sure to collect the acknowledgement receipt.

4 · Check Your Name in the Draft Roll

After July 21, confirm your name appears in the Draft Roll. If missing, apply before August 20.

For those already on the electoral roll: You only need to submit the Enumeration Form and your electoral roll extract. No additional documents are required.

How to submit online?

  • Open the voters.eci.gov.in website or the Voter Helpline app.
  • Log in with your EPIC (Voter ID) number.
  • For online eSign, your mobile number must be linked to your Voter ID and names must match.
  • Review the pre-filled form, make corrections, and submit.
Caution: Do NOT hand over original documents to anyone during enumeration. Only the form is collected at this stage. If documents are needed, you will receive a notice from the ERO later.

Required Documents

What Documents Are Needed?

Those already on the electoral roll generally do not need extra documents — submitting the Enumeration Form is sufficient. Documents are needed only if your details don't match the old roll, or if you are being newly added.

Common Problems & Solutions

What to Do If You Face Difficulties?

House was locked when BLO visitedName may be marked "not residing"
Contact the BLO and submit the completed form, or submit online at voters.eci.gov.in.
Away from home for studies or workMay be incorrectly marked as "permanently relocated"
Submit your local address proof and Enumeration Form. Confirm you are an ordinary resident of that constituency.
Did not receive the form / BLO didn't visit
Call 1950 helpline, or visit the BLO/ERO office. You can also access and submit the form online.
Name / address spelling is incorrect
Correct it on the form to match your documents. You can also correct it during the Draft Roll stage.
Name deleted from the roll after SIR
Don't panic. Re-add your name via Form 6. Apply at voters.eci.gov.in and save the reference number.
Name appears in two places
Only one entry will be kept. Request to retain your name in the constituency where you reside, and delete the other.
Important: Before removing a name from the electoral roll, a notice is generally issued and you are given the opportunity to respond or object. This is why checking the Draft Roll is crucial. For the exact procedure, contact 1950 or voters.eci.gov.in.

What the Minority Community Should Know

Important Points

  • SIR is NOT NRC. It is only an electoral roll revision. It has no connection to citizenship.
  • Filling the form has no effect on your citizenship.
  • Before a name is removed from the roll, a notice is generally issued and you have the right to object.
  • Do not believe rumours. Trust only voters.eci.gov.in or 1950 for official information.
  • Submitting the form on time and confirming your name in the Draft Roll is the most important step.
Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

I am already a registered voter. Do I need to fill the form again?
Yes. Every voter must fill and submit the Enumeration Form during SIR. However, you do not need additional documents.
What happens if I don't submit the form?
Your name may not appear in the Draft Roll. Make sure to submit before July 14.
What if I have no documents?
If you are already on the roll, no documents are needed. If you are a new applicant, try to obtain any one document from the list. If in doubt, contact the 1950 helpline or your BLO.
Can I submit online?
Yes. Via voters.eci.gov.in or the Voter Helpline app. Your mobile number must be linked to your Voter ID.
Is this free? Do I need to pay anyone?
It is completely free. Do not pay anyone. If someone asks for money, refuse and report to authorities.
