Section 10

Passion vs Career — A Balanced View

"Follow your passion" sounds inspiring, but on its own it is incomplete advice.

✗ Incomplete advice

"Just follow your passion." Passion alone, without skill or demand, can leave you struggling for years and unable to support your family.

✓ Balanced advice

  1. Identify your genuine interests.
  2. Build valuable, real-world skills.
  3. Understand what the market actually needs.
  4. Combine passion with competence and opportunity.

Passion without skill is risky.
Skill without interest can become exhausting.

Skill + Interest + Opportunity = a sustainable career

The point

Scholarships can dramatically reduce the financial barrier to education. A student from a low-income family can complete a strong degree with the right support — so explore every option before deciding a course is "too expensive."

Section 12

An Islamic Perspective on Learning

Beyond marks and jobs, Islam places great value on seeking knowledge, doing work with excellence, and serving family and community.

The Value of Knowledge

The very first revealed word was "Iqra" — Read. Seeking beneficial knowledge — worldly and religious — is encouraged and rewarded.

Responsibility to Family

Earning a halal livelihood to support your parents and family is a noble act. A steady, honest income is a form of worship and care.

Excellence (Ihsan) in Work

"Allah loves that when one does a job, he does it with excellence." Whatever you choose — engineer, nurse, trader, teacher — do it sincerely and well.

Lifelong Learning

Learning never stops at a degree. Keep improving your skills and character throughout life — for your benefit and your community's.

"Seek knowledge and act upon it with sincerity, work with excellence, and serve your family and community — and trust that effort is never wasted."
