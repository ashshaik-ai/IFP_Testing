Arabic Phrases

Useful Arabic Phrases

Essential phrases used in Islamic life and Muslim community interactions.

اَلسَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَةُ اللہِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ
As-salamu alaykum wa rahmatullahi wa barakatuh
Peace, mercy of Allah, and His blessings be upon you
The complete salam — carries the most reward
كَيْفَ حَالُكَ؟
Kayfa haaluka? (m) / Kayfa haaluki? (f)
How are you?
Common follow-up after salam
أَنَا بِخَيْر، وَالْحَمْدُ للہ
Ana bi-khayr, wal-hamdulillah
I am well, and praise be to Allah
Reply to Kayfa haaluka
مَا اسْمُكَ؟
Ma ismuka? (m) / Ma ismuki? (f)
What is your name?
Used when meeting someone
اسْمِي...
Ismi... (My name is...)
My name is... (fill your name)
To introduce yourself
مِنْ أَيْنَ أَنْتَ؟
Min ayna anta? (m) / Min ayna anti? (f)
Where are you from?
Common question at first meeting
أَنَا مِنَ الهِنْد
Ana min al-hind
I am from India
Replace with your country name
لَا أَفْهَمُ
Laa afham
I don't understand
When something is unclear
تَكَلَّمْ بِبُطْءٍ، مِنْ فَضْلِكَ
Takallam bibut'in, min fadlika
Please speak slowly
When someone speaks too quickly
شُكْرًا جَزِيلًا
Shukran jazeelan
Thank you very much
For expressing deep gratitude

Arabic gender grammar: Arabic has distinct masculine (mudhakkar) and feminine (muannath) forms. anta (you, m) and anti (you, f). This is fundamental to Arabic.

Islamic Expressions

Frequently Used Islamic Arabic Expressions

Essential expressions every Muslim should know — from daily life and worship.

إِنْ شَاءَ اللہ
In sha' Allah
If Allah wills
Said for any future plan — Quranic command (18:23-24)
اَلْحَمْدُ لِلّٰہ
Alhamdulillah
All praise is due to Allah
In all circumstances — joy, hardship, reply to how are you
مَاشَاءَ اللہ
Ma sha' Allah
What Allah has willed — how wonderful!
Admiration — also protects against evil eye
سُبْحَانَ اللہ
Subhan Allah
Glory be to Allah
Witnessing beauty of creation or great news
بِسْمِ اللہِ الرَّحْمٰنِ الرَّحِيْم
Bismillahi ar-rahmani ar-raheem
In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful
Before every important action — begins every surah
اَللہُ أَكْبَر
Allahu Akbar
Allah is the Greatest
In prayer, adhan, amazement, and joy
أَسْتَغْفِرُ اللہَ
Astaghfirullah
I seek forgiveness from Allah
After sin — Prophet ﷺ said it 70+ times daily
جَزَاكَ اللهُ خَيْرًا
Jazakallahu khayran
May Allah reward you with goodness
Deeper than 'thank you' — a prayer for their reward
إِنَّا لِلّٰہِ وَإِنَّا إِلَيْهِ رَاجِعُوْن
Inna lillahi wa inna ilayhi raji'un
Truly we belong to Allah and to Him we return
Upon hearing of death or any calamity — Quran 2:156
بَارَكَ اللهُ فِيكَ
Barakallahu feeka (m) / feeki (f)
May Allah bless you
Reply to jazakallahu khayran or standalone blessing
لَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا اللہ
La ilaha illallah
There is no deity worthy of worship except Allah
The kalimah — the most important phrase in Islam
صَلَّى اللہُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّم
Sallallahu alayhi wa sallam (ﷺ)
May Allah's peace and blessings be upon him
Said every time Prophet Muhammad ﷺ is mentioned
Daily Conversations

Converse in Arabic

Key conversations useful when speaking Arabic in the Muslim community.

هَلْ تَتَكَلَّمُ اَلْعَرَبِيَّة؟
Hal tatakallamu al-arabiyya?
Do you speak Arabic?
Confirming language
نَعَمْ، قَلِيلًا
Na'am, qaleelan
Yes, a little
Humble beginner's reply
أَيْنَ الْمَسْجِد؟
Ayna al-masjid?
Where is the mosque?
Asking for the mosque while travelling
مَتَى صَلَاةُ الْعَصْر؟
Mata salatu al-asr?
When is Asr prayer?
Ask for any prayer time — Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, Isha
هَلِ الطَّعَامُ حَلَال؟
Hal at-ta'amu halal?
Is the food halal?
At restaurants or as a guest
أَنَا مُسْلِم
Ana muslim (m) / Ana muslima (f)
I am a Muslim
To state your identity with confidence
أَنَا أَتَعَلَّمُ اَلْعَرَبِيَّة
Ana ata'allamu al-arabiyya
I am learning Arabic
Announce your Arabic learning journey

Why learn Arabic? Arabic is the language of the Quran. Learning it enables you to read the Quran with comprehension, understand what you hear in salah, and connect with the global Muslim community. Every step is an act of worship.
