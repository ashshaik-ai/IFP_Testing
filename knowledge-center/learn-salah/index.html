Motivation

Why Learn Salah?

Salah is the second pillar of Islam and a servant's daily connection with Allah. Learning it correctly is the most important step in a Muslim's life.

🕌

The Pillar of Islam

Salah is the first practical pillar of Islam after faith. The very first deed we will be questioned about on Judgement Day is the prayer.

إِنَّ الصَّلَاةَ كَانَتْ عَلَى الْمُؤْمِنِينَ كِتَابًا مَّوْقُوتًا
🤲

Direct Connection with Allah

Five times a day, the worshipper speaks directly with their Lord through prayer. It is peace for the heart and strength for the soul.

وَأَقِمِ الصَّلَاةَ لِذِكْرِي
🌿

Character & Discipline

Prayer restrains from wrongdoing and indecency, structures the day, and instils true discipline and self-control.

إِنَّ الصَّلَاةَ تَنْهَىٰ عَنِ الْفَحْشَاءِ وَالْمُنكَرِ

The Prayer Steps
١
Takbir · التكبير
Standing, raise the hands to the ears
اللَّهُ أَكْبَر
Allāhu Akbar
“Allah is the Greatest” — the prayer begins with this Takbir.
٢
Qiyam · القيام
Standing, right hand over the left
سُبْحَانَكَ اللَّهُمَّ وَبِحَمْدِكَ
Subḥānaka Allāhumma wa biḥamdik
“Glory be to You, O Allah, and praise be to You” — the opening supplication (Thana).
٣
Surah Al-Fatihah · الفاتحة
Standing, recite Al-Fatihah
الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ
Al-ḥamdu lillāhi rabbil-ʿālamīn
“All praise is for Allah, Lord of all worlds” — Al-Fatihah is recited in every unit.
٤
Ruku · الركوع
Bowing, hands on the knees
سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْعَظِيم
Subḥāna Rabbiyal-ʿAẓīm
“Glory to my Lord, the Most Great” — said three times.
٥
Sujood · السجود
Prostration — forehead and nose on the ground
سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْأَعْلَى
Subḥāna Rabbiyal-Aʿlā
“Glory to my Lord, the Most High” — said three times; the closest a servant is to Allah.
٦
Jalsa · الجلسة
Sitting between the two prostrations
رَبِّ اغْفِرْ لِي
Rabbighfir lī
“My Lord, forgive me” — then make the second prostration.
٧
Tashahhud · التشهد
Sitting, raising the index finger
التَّحِيَّاتُ لِلَّهِ وَالصَّلَوَاتُ وَالطَّيِّبَاتُ
At-taḥiyyātu lillāhi waṣ-ṣalawātu waṭ-ṭayyibāt
“All greetings, prayers and good things are for Allah” — followed by the salutation on the Prophet ﷺ.
٨
Salam · السلام
Turning the head right, then left
السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَةُ اللَّه
As-salāmu ʿalaykum wa raḥmatullāh
“Peace and mercy of Allah be upon you” — this completes the prayer.
🎯

Correct Your Mistakes

Common Mistakes

Mistakes many people make unknowingly — recognise them and correct each one the right way.

💧

Wudu Mistakes

✗Leaving body parts dry (dry heels, dry elbows).
✓Make sure water reaches every part of each limb.
✗Wasting water and being excessive.
✓Use moderately — no more than three washes.
🕌

Salah Mistakes

✗Rushing between positions without stillness.
✓Pause in each position until the body settles (tuma'ninah).
✗Moving ahead of the imam.
✓Follow the imam — move only after he does.
🧍

Posture Mistakes

✗Not straightening the back fully in ruku.
✓Keep the back and head level in ruku.
✗The seven limbs not touching the ground in sujood.
✓Place forehead, nose, both hands, knees and toes on the ground.
📿

Recitation Mistakes

✗Mispronouncing words so the meaning changes.
✓Recite slowly and clearly with the correct letter sounds.
✗Skipping Al-Fatihah or reciting it too fast.
✓Recite Al-Fatihah fully and clearly in every unit.
💭

Concentration Mistakes

✗Letting the mind wander; praying mechanically.
✓Stand with khushu, reflecting on the meaning of what you say.
✗Looking around or fidgeting with clothes or phone.
✓Keep the gaze at the place of prostration and stay calm.
